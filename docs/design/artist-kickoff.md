# CORE Quest — Artist kickoff

A conversation starter, not a brief. Two refining sessions on my end are still to come (character design, then art direction); this doc just shows you where we are.

## What CORE Quest is

A productivity PWA dressed as an RPG. Real-life tasks earn action points for a fixed team of 5; the player spends those points in turn-based combat against monsters living in portal worlds that open in modern-day settings. Personal app — I'm the only user — so we can iterate fast and break things.

## Where we are

We'd previously locked **HD-2D pixel art** as the most ambitious style I could execute solo (refs: Octopath Traveler, Sea of Stars). With you in the picture, we're re-opening to **Unicorn Overlord-style** — that style was ruled out a few weeks ago purely as a capacity call, not a taste call. Vanguard (the Warrior) has a v1 pixel render done — the *design* intent is locked (modern tactical operator + fantasy plate armor, gold-rune pauldron, two-handed greatsword, earnest-heroic tone), but the rendering is up for re-trial. I'll send the pixel reference alongside this.

## The 5 archetypes — exploratory

These are still placeholders pulled from the power set and the metaphor — actual character design is a separate next-step session. Treat the below as where my head is, not as a brief.

- **Warrior / The Vanguard** — Health quests. The body, the will, the act. *Crashing Strike* (gold + red, heavy kinetic). Vanguard v1 is the design anchor: tactical operator fused with fantasy plate. **Design locked, rendering open.**
- **Mage** — Cognitive / learning quests. The mind, predictable power, the planner. *Calculated Burn* (cyan + arcane purple, controlled beam). Could be a scholar-coded student, could be a quiet strategist who runs the math. Open.
- **Rogue** — Money / strategic quests. Edge, opportunism, finesse. *Edge Work* (deep navy + cyan flicker, fast). Could be a street-smart hustler, could be a polished operator. Open.
- **Diplomat** — Relationships. Warmth, presence, the connector. *Rallying Word* (warm amber + cream, soft glow — buffs allies, debuffs enemies, no direct damage). Could be a charismatic professional, could be a quiet steady presence. Open.
- **Scholar** — Creative / reflective. Insight, patience, observation. *Insight* (cyan + cream, lens-like — reveals weakness for the next attack). Artist, writer, or contemplative — open.

Each Persona power is mechanically skin-agnostic, so weapons can be sword / shotgun / chainsaw / whatever fits the character. We'll figure that out together once the characters are real people.

## Art ideas for the bigger canvas

Things UO-style enables that pixel couldn't:

- **Rich facial expression.** The team has a faith/belief arc baked into the game (skeptical → trusting); expressions carry the story.
- **Modern + fantasy fusion in silhouette.** Read at a glance: present-day clothing + fantasy gear. Vanguard's tactical helmet over fantasy pauldrons is the canonical example.
- **Painterly equipment storytelling.** Rune-etched plates, a single iconic talisman, modern straps. Detail in service of character, not maximalist.
- **Color anchors from the existing app palette:** navy backdrop, gold accent, cyan/teal energy, HP red, MP blue — accents, not dominating constraints.
- **Tone: earnest-heroic.** UO and 13 Sentinels are good blends; sincere adventure rather than grimdark or ironic.
- **Phone-readable.** It's a PWA on mobile — characters need to work at small sizes.

## What I'm *not* asking for yet

No animations, no environments, no alt-weapon skins. Just: what does your eye do with these archetypes once we hand you real characters? Sketches, mood, "this is the direction" — not finished work.

## Next steps

Two design sessions on my end first — characters, then a round of prompt-art to dial in the look. After those land we'll come back to you with a proper brief. In the meantime: anything in the above jumping out at you?
